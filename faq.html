Frequently Asked Questions

What is ASTRA?

ASTRA is a software concept that tracks satellites and debris, predicts close approaches, and helps assess collision risk in Earth orbit.

What problem is ASTRA trying to solve?

ASTRA focuses on the growing risk of collisions in crowded orbits, where even one impact can create thousands of debris fragments that threaten other missions.

How does ASTRA track satellites and debris?

ASTRA ingests orbital data, such as catalogs and orbital elements, and uses it to maintain an up to date picture of where satellites and debris are in space.

What is a conjunction?

A conjunction is a predicted close approach between two objects in orbit, such as a satellite and a piece of debris, where the distance between them becomes small enough to be a concern.

How does ASTRA predict close approaches?

Using orbital models, ASTRA propagates the orbits of objects forward in time and checks for times when their paths pass close to one another.

How does ASTRA estimate collision risk?

ASTRA looks at factors like miss distance, relative speed, and uncertainty in the orbit data to give each event a simple collision risk score.

Does ASTRA actually move satellites?

No. ASTRA does not control satellites directly. It is meant to help human operators decide when a maneuver might be needed and what options are safer.

Where does ASTRA get its data from?

In this school project, ASTRA uses example orbital data and assumed inputs. A real system could use public satellite catalogs and tracking data from sensors.

Can ASTRA work with real space agencies in the future?

The current version is a concept and demonstration, but the ideas behind ASTRA are similar to tools that real operators could use to manage collision risk.

Why is space debris such a big issue?

Every piece of debris travels at very high speed. Even a small fragment can damage or destroy a satellite, and collisions can generate more debris that stays in orbit for years.

What happens if two objects collide in orbit?

A collision can create a cloud of fragments that spread out along the orbit and increase the risk for many other satellites over time.

How is the ASTRA visualization used?

The visualization lets users see orbits, track close approaches over time, and inspect which encounters are more dangerous in a clear, visual way.

Does ASTRA run in real time?

In this project, ASTRA runs on preloaded data and simulated time steps. A real implementation could update more frequently as new tracking data arrives.

Who is ASTRA designed for?

ASTRA is aimed at mission planners, satellite operators, and students who want a clearer view of how conjunctions and collision risks develop in orbit.

Is ASTRA a finished product or a school project?

This version of ASTRA is a student project built to show the concept of automated tracking, prediction, and risk assessment.

What are some limitations of ASTRA?

ASTRA uses simplified models, limited data, and runs on a small scale compared to real systems. It is meant for learning and demonstration, not for live satellite control.

How does ASTRA help make space more sustainable?

By highlighting risky encounters earlier, ASTRA supports decisions that can avoid collisions and reduce the growth of debris in key orbital regions.

Could ASTRA be expanded in the future?

Yes. Future versions could use more detailed data, better physics models, and closer integration with real mission planning tools.

Do I need any special software to use ASTRA?

For this project, ASTRA is presented as a website with a visualization, so it can be viewed in a modern web browser.

How can I learn more about ASTRA?

You can explore the About page, try the visualization, and read the other pages in this site to see how the system works and what problem it targets.
