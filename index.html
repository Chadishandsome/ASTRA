Automated Satellite Tracking and Risk Assessment (ASTRA) – Our Proposal

Context

Earth’s orbit is filling up with active satellites, dead hardware, and debris. Every new launch increases the chance of close approaches that can damage or destroy critical space systems used for communication, GPS, and weather monitoring.

Problem

Operators receive huge amounts of tracking data but have limited time to decide which conjunctions are actually dangerous and which satellites need to maneuver before a collision creates even more debris.

ASTRA’s Idea

ASTRA is a software system that ingests orbital data for satellites and debris, predicts their future positions, and automatically flags high-risk close approaches. It assigns a collision risk score to each event and suggests simple avoidance maneuvers that minimize fuel use and mission impact.

Why It Matters

By reducing the chance of debris-creating collisions, ASTRA helps protect vital space infrastructure and keeps low Earth orbit usable for future science, navigation, and communication missions.

Project Focus

Preventing satellite collisions in an increasingly crowded orbit.

Key Outputs

Risk scores, flagged conjunctions, and basic avoidance suggestions.

Data Sources

Uses satellite catalogs, debris tracking data, and orbital elements.

Technology Stack

Predictive models, visualization tools, and automated scoring systems.

Interactive Conjunction Visualization

The ASTRA visualization lets you see satellites and debris moving in orbit, forecast close approaches, and inspect collision risk for individual events. It turns raw tracking data into something you can actually explore and understand.

Use it to step through time, focus on a specific satellite, and see how different avoidance decisions change the risk around it.
